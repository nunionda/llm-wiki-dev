# Comparative Analysis: Pillars of Film Criticism

This document analyzes the "Masters" to provide specific writing styles for the [[film-critic-thesis]].

## 1. Roger Ebert: The Conversational Humanist
*   **Philosophy**: "It's not what a movie is about, it's how it is about it."
*   **Writing Style**: First-person, accessible, storytelling-centric. He often used the "Theater Experience" as a narrative frame.
*   **Lesson for Blogging**: Connect with the reader's *feeling*. Don't be condescending.

## 2. André Bazin: The Ontological Realist
*   **Philosophy**: The camera should capture reality with minimal interference.
*   **Writing Style**: Analytical, philosophical, and intensely focused on [[technical-lexicon]]. Championed the "Long Take" and "Depth of Field."
*   **Lesson for Blogging**: Explain the *craft*. Show the reader how a specific lens choice creates a specific psychological effect.

## 3. Lee Dong-jin: The Subtext Architect
*   **Philosophy**: Modern cinema is a complex web of cultural and psychological symbols.
*   **Writing Style**: Precise, methodical, and often literary. He breaks down the "Structure of the Story" to reveal hidden meanings.
*   **Lesson for Blogging**: Look for the *patterns*. Connect the film to books, history, and social trends to provide "Value-add" knowledge.
