# Film Critic's Technical Lexicon

To move beyond "I liked it," a professional blogger must master these terms.

## 1. Visual Language (Mise-en-scène)
- **Deep Focus**: Keeping objects in both the foreground and background in sharp focus (favored by Bazin).
- **Dutch Angle**: A tilted shot used to create tension or a sense of disorientation.
- **Rule of Thirds**: Compositional rule for placing subjects in a visually pleasing way.
- **Color Palette**: The specific set of colors used to evoke mood (e.g., "Neon Noir").
- **Verticality ([[수직성]])**: Use of vertical space (stairs, heights) to symbolize social hierarchies and class tension (e.g., *Parasite*).

## 2. Narrative Vocabulary
- **Diegetic Sound**: Sound that originates within the film's world (e.g., characters listening to a radio).
- **MacGuffin**: An object or goal that drives the plot but may be unimportant in itself (Hitchcock term).
- **Pacing**: The rhythm of the film—how fast or slow the information is delivered.
- **Clear Weaving ([[명징한 직조]])**: A meticulous arrangement of cinematic elements where every symbol and choice is logically connected to the core theme (Lee Dong-jin's term).

## 3. Critical Terms
- **Auteur Theory**: The idea that the director is the "author" of the film with a consistent style.
- **Cinematic Tension**: The psychological effect generated by editing and sound choices.
- **Class Fable (계급 우화)**: A narrative that uses metaphor to critique social stratification and power dynamics.
